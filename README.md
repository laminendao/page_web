## About Me

I am a PhD researcher in **Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)** and **Statistical Learning**, currently working at the intersection of interpretable machine learning, time series analysis, and predictive maintenance. My research focuses on evaluating and improving XAI methods (post-hoc approaches like SHAP, LIME, L2X) for high-dimensional multivariate time series, with applications in **Remaining Useful Life (RUL)** prediction and industrial predictive maintenance.

- **Current position** (2025–present): ATER (Attaché Temporaire d’Enseignement et de Recherche) at **Conservatoire National des Arts et Métiers (CNAM)**, Paris & Nouvelle-Aquitaine
- **PhD** (2022–2025): Contractual doctoral researcher at **LINEACT CESI**, Nanterre, France
- **Research interests**: XAI evaluation, interpretable dimensionality reduction, time series segmentation, robust feature selection, reproducible ML workflows

## Research Interests

- Quantitative evaluation of XAI methods (impact of preprocessing and model complexity)
- Interpretable approaches for multivariate time series forecasting
- Remaining Useful Life (RUL) prediction in predictive maintenance
- Dimensionality reduction and feature selection for high-dimensional data
- Reproducible and interpretable machine learning pipelines
